The "Adapt Yourself" Era

The prevailing wisdom

  • Prompt engineering courses
  • Structured input templates
  • "Talk to AI like this..."
  • Change your workflows to fit the tool

Acknowledge: this works!

It's not wrong. I've done this at work —
changed processes to work better with AI tooling.

But what gets lost?

The cost

You lose the messy, creative, nonlinear thinking
that makes humans good at ideation.

The irony: AI is great at handling unstructured input,
yet we're told to give it structured input.

I think best with a pen.

Doodles, arrows, scribbles, brain dumps.

Or talking it out loud on a walk.

That's not going away.

The Inversion

Don't adapt yourself to AI.
Adapt AI to your existing flow.

Why now?

  • Multimodal AI can read handwriting
  • It can interpret sketches and messy layouts
  • Voice input turns stream-of-consciousness into structure

The reMarkable isn't a productivity tool.
It's a thinking tool.

What Makes This Work

The ingredients

  • Multimodal AI reads messy handwriting (surprisingly well)
  • Voice input via Claude mobile — verbal brain dumps on a walk
  • Cowork mode — iterative refinement, not one-shot

The key insight

Brain dumps contain more signal
than carefully typed prompts.

Because you don't self-censor.

When it doesn't work

  • Illegibility has limits — truly chaotic scrawl can confuse it
  • Network dependency — rM cloud sync can be slow
  • Manual export friction — still requires PDF → upload → prompt
